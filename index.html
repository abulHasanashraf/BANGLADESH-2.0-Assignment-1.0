Pillars of "Bangladesh 2.0"

Innovation

Bangladesh 2.0 thrives on technological advancements, fostering a digital economy with innovations in ICT, fintech, and agriculture, improving efficiency and accessibility

Sustainability

Focusing on eco-friendly practices, Bangladesh 2.0 integrates renewable energy, climate change mitigation, and sustainable development to protect resources for future generations.

Infrastructure

Modern infrastructure projects, such as highways, bridges, and ports, enhance connectivity, support economic growth, and improve the quality of life in Bangladesh 2.0.

Empowerment

Bangladesh 2.0 prioritizes gender equality, education, and healthcare, empowering individuals and communities to thrive, fostering a more inclusive and equitable society.

The Vision of Future

Bangladesh 2.0 aims to redefine the nation's trajectory by embracing cutting-edge technology, promoting inclusive growth, and ensuring sustainability. With initiatives in renewable energy, digital transformation, and robust infrastructure, the country is set to become a global model of development.

Digital Transformation

The future will be driven by digital transformation, with technology enhancing connectivity, business efficiency, and education, creating a smarter, more interconnected world for all.

Green Energy

A sustainable future depends on green energy solutions like solar, wind, and hydropower, reducing carbon footprints and ensuring long-term environmental health while meeting global energy needs.

Modern Infrastructure

Future infrastructure will prioritize smart cities, efficient transportation, and resilient buildings, supporting economic growth, improving quality of life, and adapting to environmental challenges.

Muhammad Yunus, the Nobel Peace Prize laureate and founder of Grameen Bank, has inspired millions with his quotes on social entrepreneurship and poverty alleviation. One of his notable quotes is: “Poverty is not created by poor people; poverty is created by the system, by the way we design our societies. Poor people are simply victims of that system." Yunus emphasizes that poverty is not a result of individual failure but rather a consequence of societal structures. He also said, “I believe that we can create a world of peace and dignity by providing opportunities for people to work together in harmony.” His vision calls for creating a world where everyone has access to the tools needed for success, particularly in empowering individuals through microfinance. Yunus advocates for social businesses designed to address social issues while remaining financially sustainable, transforming lives without exploiting others.
